# AI Training Notice - Ethical Position on Commercial Closed-Source AI

**This notice is a public ethical statement by the author. It is not a license, does not modify or supersede the Apache License 2.0, and imposes no additional legal restrictions. The project remains fully licensed under Apache 2.0.**

---

## 1. Why This Notice Exists

This project was released under the Apache License 2.0 because the author believes it should remain genuinely open-source: reusable, modifiable, redistributable, and accessible to developers, researchers, educators, and small teams under a standard OSI-approved license.

At the same time, the author strongly objects to the growing practice of using open-source community work to train, evaluate, optimize, or improve commercially closed-source artificial intelligence systems.

The author specifically wished to restrict this kind of use while preserving the ordinary freedoms associated with open-source software.

However, after extensive research, the author concluded that no currently established license framework adequately supports this goal while remaining compatible with the Open Source Definition maintained by the Open Source Initiative (OSI).

Under the OSI framework, licenses may not discriminate against particular fields of endeavor or categories of use. As a result, a license that legally prohibits commercial closed-source AI training cannot simultaneously remain an OSI-approved open-source license.

Because of this unresolved gap, the author chose to retain Apache 2.0 and publish this separate ethical notice instead.

This notice is therefore not a legal mechanism, but a moral and philosophical declaration: a public explanation of the author’s values, intended to guide responsible use and to communicate explicit ethical opposition to commercial closed-source AI exploitation.

---

## 2. Transitional Licensing Position

The author expects that future licensing models, ethical licensing frameworks, or community standards may eventually emerge to address the specific problem of open-source work being absorbed into commercially closed-source AI systems.

The current combination — Apache 2.0 together with this ethical notice — should therefore be understood as an interim approach.

It preserves standard open-source freedoms while clearly expressing the author’s ethical boundary regarding commercial closed-source AI use.

If a future license or licensing framework becomes available that better reflects these principles in a legally coherent and broadly understandable way, the author may choose to adopt such a license for future versions of this project.

Any future licensing change will aim to remain as close as reasonably possible to the spirit of the current arrangement:

- broad freedom for ordinary software use;
- explicit support for open-source AI development;
- tolerance for non-commercial scientific research;
- strong opposition to commercial closed-source AI exploitation.

For clarity, any future licensing changes would apply only to future releases or future contributions unless otherwise explicitly stated. Existing versions already released under Apache 2.0 remain available under Apache 2.0.

## 3. The Author’s Ethical Position

The author does not consent, does not grant ethical approval, and expresses the strongest possible ethical objection to the use of this project for any purpose that primarily supports, accelerates, improves, or benefits commercially closed-source or proprietary artificial intelligence systems.

This objection includes, but is not limited to:

- training, fine-tuning, distilling, evaluating, benchmarking, or otherwise improving AI systems whose weights, architecture, or training methodology are not meaningfully public;
- incorporating this project into datasets, benchmarks, or evaluation pipelines intended primarily to improve commercially closed-source AI systems;
- embedding this project into products, services, APIs, or infrastructure whose core purpose is to facilitate commercial proprietary AI training or inference;
- using this project as infrastructure for proprietary industrial autonomy, digital twin decision systems, or closed operational intelligence platforms whose functionality depends on non-transparent AI systems developed primarily for private competitive advantage.

The critical distinction is commercial proprietary AI.

If the resulting system exists primarily to generate private competitive advantage while withholding transparency, reproducibility, or meaningful public access, the author considers such use fundamentally contrary to the spirit and goals of this project.

### 3.1 Synthetic Data Generation

The author's ethical objection explicitly includes the use of this project to build simulation pipelines, synthetic environments, telemetry capture systems, or automated recording workflows intended primarily to generate synthetic datasets for commercial closed-source AI training, evaluation, optimization, or benchmarking.

This includes large-scale simulated robotics, autonomous systems, embodied AI, sim-to-real learning workflows, or industrial automation pipelines whose outputs primarily benefit non-transparent proprietary AI systems.

### 3.2 Protocol and Serialization Extraction

The author's ethical objection also applies to the extraction, reuse, or adaptation of project-specific protocol definitions, Unity-oriented Protobuf schemas, MCAP serialization logic, telemetry routing infrastructure, or related implementation details when such extraction is performed primarily to support commercial closed-source AI training or inference pipelines.

---

## 4. Explicit Exception for Non-Commercial Research

The author explicitly waives the ethical objection stated above for closed-source AI systems used solely for non-commercial scientific, academic, educational, archival, or independent research purposes.

This exception applies when:

- the work is not directed, owned, or substantially controlled by a commercial entity;
- the resulting system is not integrated into a commercial product or revenue-generating service;
- the purpose is scientific inquiry, education, experimentation, reproducibility research, or personal non-profit exploration.

The author recognizes that non-commercial research — even when not fully open — contributes differently to public knowledge than commercial proprietary AI exploitation.

This exception is intentional and should not be interpreted as a loophole.

If such a system later becomes commercialized or integrated into a proprietary commercial offering, the author considers the ethical objection described in this notice to apply fully from that point forward.

---

## 5. Open-Source AI Is Explicitly Encouraged

The author explicitly welcomes and encourages the use of this project for the training, evaluation, fine-tuning, or advancement of genuinely open-source AI systems.

For the purposes of this notice, open-source AI systems are systems released under OSI-approved licenses with publicly available model weights, architecture, training methodology, and relevant training code sufficient for meaningful public study, modification, reproduction, and redistribution.

Whether commercial or non-commercial, transparent and reproducible AI development serves the public good far more effectively than closed, non-accountable AI systems.

## 6. Rationale

This project — Unity2Foxglove — was created to democratize advanced simulation debugging, real-time data streaming, telemetry inspection, robotics visualization, and MCAP recording workflows. Its purpose is to provide individual developers, researchers, educators, and small teams with capabilities that are often available only inside large organizations with substantial internal infrastructure.The democratization of robotics debugging and telemetry infrastructure is not merely about convenience or productivity — it is also about safety, auditability, and transparency.

When commercial entities use open-source simulation and debugging tools to improve proprietary autonomous systems while withholding the resulting safety characteristics, behavioral limitations, or decision-making logic, they create a form of "safety black box" that benefits from community labor without enabling meaningful public scrutiny or accountability.

The author considers this especially concerning in domains involving robotics, autonomy, physical systems, embodied AI, or sim-to-real deployment.Using open community infrastructure to improve commercially closed-source AI systems directly conflicts with the mission of this project.

In the author’s view, such use extracts value from the commons while returning little transparency, accountability, reciprocity, or shared benefit. The author therefore considers commercial proprietary AI use of this project to be:

- ethically unacceptable;
- exploitative toward open-source maintainers and contributors;
- contrary to the collaborative spirit of open-source development;
- harmful to long-term technological transparency and public accountability.

The author distinguishes this clearly from non-commercial scientific research, which serves fundamentally different social and educational purposes.

## 7. Attribution and Metadata Request

The author encourages users who publicly distribute datasets, recordings, telemetry streams, or simulation outputs generated using this project — including MCAP recordings or derived synthetic datasets — to preserve attribution, provenance information, or metadata references to this project whenever reasonably possible.

Maintaining such attribution helps the broader community understand which open-source infrastructure contributed to the creation of public datasets and improves transparency regarding how such data may later be used.

## 8. Community and Social Response

Because this notice is not part of the software license, it does not create legal penalties or revoke rights granted under Apache 2.0.

Anyone remains legally free to use the software under the terms of Apache 2.0.

However, the absence of legal restriction does not imply ethical approval.

If a commercial entity knowingly uses this project in ways fundamentally opposed to the ethical position expressed here, the author may choose to:

- publicly state opposition to such use;
- decline collaboration, support, sponsorship, or endorsement;
- refuse participation in events, partnerships, or promotional activities connected to such entities;
- encourage broader discussion within the open-source community regarding exploitative relationships between open-source labor and closed proprietary AI systems.

The author strongly requests that organizations unwilling to respect these principles either avoid using this project for commercial proprietary AI purposes or instead choose to release their AI systems in a genuinely open and reproducible form.
